Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical Robot Vacuum last?

With proper care and maintenance, a quality Robot Vacuum can last anywhere from 3 to 7 years depending on usage frequency and the specific model. Higher-end models with better components tend to have a longer lifespan. Regular cleaning and following the manufacturer's maintenance guidelines will help maximize longevity.

Do I need the latest model or is last year's version good enough?

Unless the latest model introduces a significant technology upgrade, last year's version is often the better value. Manufacturers tend to make incremental improvements year over year, and you can usually find the previous generation at a substantial discount while still getting excellent performance.

Can a Robot Vacuum really make a difference for home offices?

What should I check before buying a Robot Vacuum online?

What is the difference between budget and premium Robot Vacuums?

Budget models typically use lower-cost materials, have fewer features, and may lack the refinement of premium options. Premium models invest in better engineering, higher-quality components, and more rigorous testing. The gap has narrowed in recent years, but premium options still offer a noticeably better experience for frequent users.

How much should I spend on a good Robot Vacuum?

For a reliable Robot Vacuum that will serve you well for years, plan to spend between $200 and $1500. At this price point, you get solid build quality, good performance, and dependable customer support. Entry-level options under $200 often cut corners that affect the experience.
